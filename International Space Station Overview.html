What is the International Space Station?


The International Space Station, also known as the ISS, is a laboratory and spacecraft orbiting around the Earth. The International Space Station is a place where astronauts and cosmonauts live and study whilst in space. Five different Space Agencies, NASA, Roscosmos, JAXA, the ESA and the CSA, use the International Space Station to learn more about space by conducting experiments relating to space environment and in the subjects of biology, physics, astronomy, meteorology , and other fields.

The construction of the International Space Station began in November 1998, when a Russian rocket brought up the first piece of the Space Station. More rockets from many different space agencies have brought up more and more pieces of the space station since. Construction of the station was completed in 2011. Since November 2000, the International Space Station has been continually occupied. The Space Station has made it possible for humans to live in space for an extended period of time.

The International Space Station is made of many different sections, or nodes. each node was brought up from earth by a different rocket, and they were pieced together inspace. There are many different types of nodes on the International Space Station. There are nodes for laboratories, bathrooms, sleeping areas, fitness for the astronauts and cosmonauts, airlocks for exiting the Space Station, and docking ports for spaceships, as well as a small room called the Cupola. The Cupola is a place wehere astronauts, scientists and cosmonauts can study and enjoy the view at the Earth below.
